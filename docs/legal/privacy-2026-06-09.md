# ORSA Privacy Policy

Version: 2026-06-09

This Privacy Policy explains how ORSA collects, uses, stores, protects, shares, and deletes information. It is written for the current ORSA service model: an AI health companion for symptom intake, document review, health information organization, reminders, and user-controlled profile context.

This policy is a product/legal baseline and must be reviewed by qualified counsel before production launch in any jurisdiction.

## 1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to ORSA websites, apps, APIs, chat workflows, account flows, upload workflows, settings, reminders, profile features, and support workflows that link to or present this policy.

It does not apply to third-party websites, app stores, identity providers, healthcare providers, emergency services, or other services that ORSA does not control.

## 2. Controller / Operator

The ORSA service operator is responsible for determining how ORSA processes information unless a separate written agreement says another party acts as controller, processor, covered entity, business associate, or service provider.

Production deployments must replace this section with the legal entity name, postal address, privacy contact, support email, data-protection officer if applicable, and representative if required by local law.

## 3. Information ORSA May Collect

ORSA may collect and process the following categories of information:

- Account information: email address, username, display name, user identifier, account creation time, last login time, account status.
- Authentication information: password hash, OAuth identifiers, provider identifiers, access tokens, refresh/session metadata, login attempts, sign-out events, and security metadata.
- Legal acceptance records: terms version, privacy version, consent version, timestamps, user identifier, document hashes or references, and audit records.
- Chat content: messages, symptoms, questions, answers, timestamps, thread titles, conversation IDs, deleted/restored status, and model response metadata.
- Health-related information: symptoms, onset, severity, vitals, medications, allergies, risk factors, health history, pregnancy-related information, mental-health concerns, lab values, document findings, and care-navigation context the user provides.
- Uploaded files and media: PDFs, images, screenshots, camera uploads, scans, file names, file types, file sizes, extracted text, OCR/vision summaries, analysis status, unreadable/failure reasons, and upload timestamps.
- Health profile data: optional profile summary, workflow boundary, memory consent status, extracted profile items, edits, last extraction time, and audit events.
- Reminder and notification data: reminder text, timing, status, delivery metadata, follow-up records, and notification state.
- Location information: country, region/state, city, approximate IP-derived location, and browser geolocation when the user grants permission.
- Device and usage information: IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, language, theme, timestamps, page or feature usage, performance logs, error logs, security events, and rate-limit metadata.
- Preferences: language, theme, session presence, upload usage count, optional profile/memory settings, and other app state.
- Support information: messages to support, bug reports, screenshots, diagnostics, and contact details.

ORSA may also process de-identified, aggregated, or anonymized information where permitted.

## 4. Sensitive Health Information

ORSA is designed to process health-related information provided by users. This information may be sensitive. Users should not submit information they do not want ORSA to process under this policy and the applicable consent documents.

ORSA does not intentionally collect genetic data, biometric templates, payment card data, government ID numbers, or information about children unless a feature, user upload, or user message includes it. If such information is provided, ORSA may process it only as needed to provide, secure, support, or comply with the service.

## 5. How ORSA Collects Information

ORSA collects information when users:

- Create an account or sign in.
- Accept legal documents or change consent settings.
- Send messages or respond to questions.
- Upload files or images.
- Enable, disable, or edit memory/profile settings.
- Create reminders or notifications.
- Use the app, browser, API, or support channels.
- Grant browser permissions such as location or camera/file access.

ORSA may also receive information from service providers that help operate authentication, security, analytics, support, and AI inference features.

## 6. How ORSA Uses Information

ORSA uses information to:

- Provide chat, symptom intake, triage-support, report-review, and care-navigation workflows.
- Read, extract, summarize, and explain uploaded documents or images.
- Maintain conversation history, search, restore, and deletion workflows.
- Operate optional health profile and memory features when consent is enabled.
- Apply user settings, language, theme, consent status, upload limits, and reminders.
- Authenticate users and preserve sessions.
- Secure accounts, prevent abuse, rate-limit usage, detect errors, and audit sensitive actions.
- Improve reliability, quality, accessibility, and product performance.
- Provide support, troubleshoot issues, and respond to requests.
- Comply with legal obligations, enforce terms, investigate misuse, and protect rights.
- Generate de-identified or aggregated operational insights where permitted.

## 7. AI and Model Processing

ORSA may send user messages, attachment summaries, extracted document text, profile context, and workflow state to AI systems and model providers to generate responses, summaries, extraction output, and triage-support guidance.

ORSA may use configured text, document, OCR, image, and vision AI services.

ORSA should not use stored profile context as clinical evidence. Profile context is intended only for user-approved personalization and workflow boundaries.

ORSA should not use user health data to train foundation models unless the user has been clearly told and has given any legally required consent. Provider-side retention and training controls must be verified in production contracts and settings.

AI outputs can be wrong. Users should verify important information with qualified professionals.

## 8. Legal Bases and Consent

Depending on jurisdiction, ORSA may rely on one or more legal bases, including:

- User consent for optional memory/profile extraction, certain health-data processing, location access, and other optional features.
- Contract necessity to create accounts and provide requested service features.
- Legitimate interests where permitted for security, abuse prevention, service operation, diagnostics, and product reliability.
- Legal obligation for compliance, audit, breach notification, and lawful requests.
- Vital interests in limited circumstances where processing may be needed to protect life or safety.

Users can withdraw optional consent where the product provides controls. Withdrawal does not necessarily delete data already processed where retention is permitted or required.

## 9. HIPAA, Health Apps, and Regulated Deployments

ORSA is not intended to act as a HIPAA covered entity or business associate unless a written agreement expressly says so.

A direct-to-consumer health app may fall outside HIPAA in some scenarios, while other privacy, consumer protection, breach-notification, state, national, or international rules may still apply.

If ORSA is provided by or on behalf of a healthcare provider, payer, employer, school, government entity, or regulated organization, additional notices, agreements, configuration requirements, and user rights may apply.

## 10. Sharing and Disclosure

ORSA may disclose information to:

- Service providers that help ORSA operate account, database, hosting, security, support, analytics, monitoring, and error-reporting features.
- Authentication providers.
- AI/model providers for text generation, document extraction, OCR, image analysis, and vision processing.
- Professional advisors, insurers, auditors, and legal counsel.
- Authorities, courts, regulators, or law enforcement when legally required or necessary to protect rights, safety, and security.
- Successors in connection with merger, acquisition, financing, restructuring, or sale of assets, subject to appropriate protections.

ORSA does not sell personal health information. ORSA should not share personal health information for cross-context behavioral advertising unless a separate, explicit, legally compliant consent and opt-out framework is implemented.

## 11. Third-Party Providers

ORSA may use third-party providers whose availability, privacy terms, data-processing terms, retention, and model-training policies may change. Production deployments must maintain appropriate provider records and data-processing agreements where required.

## 12. Data Storage

ORSA stores user information in a database.

ORSA may also keep operational records needed for account access, security, auditability, support, legal compliance, and service continuity.

## 13. Retention

ORSA retains information for as long as needed to provide the service, comply with law, resolve disputes, enforce terms, protect security, and preserve audit records.

Typical retention categories may include:

- Account records: while the account is active and for a reasonable period afterward.
- Legal acceptance records: as long as needed to prove acceptance and comply with legal obligations.
- Conversation history and uploads: until deleted by the user or according to configured retention rules, subject to legal needs.
- Optional profile data: until disabled, edited, deleted, or otherwise removed according to product controls and retention rules.
- Security records: for a limited operational period unless needed for investigation or compliance.

Production deployments must define exact retention periods.

## 14. Security

ORSA uses administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect information, such as:

- Encryption in transit.
- Authentication and session protections.
- Password hashing for email/password accounts.
- Access controls and least-privilege service boundaries.
- Audit records for sensitive settings and legal acceptance.
- Upload limits and file validation.
- Monitoring, logging, and abuse prevention.

No system is perfectly secure. ORSA cannot guarantee that unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or loss will never occur.

## 15. Breach and Incident Notification

If ORSA discovers a security incident involving personal information or identifiable health information, ORSA will assess the incident and provide notifications where required by applicable law, contract, or regulation.

Depending on the deployment and legal classification, obligations may include notifying affected users, regulators, the FTC, media, healthcare entities, or other parties.

Production deployments must define incident response, breach-risk assessment, notification timelines, and responsible contacts.

## 16. User Choices and Rights

Depending on location and configuration, users may have rights to:

- Access personal information.
- Correct inaccurate information.
- Delete information.
- Export or receive a portable copy.
- Withdraw optional consent.
- Object to or restrict certain processing.
- Opt out of certain sharing or marketing where applicable.
- Appeal a denied request where required.
- Complain to a privacy or data-protection authority.

Requests may be subject to identity verification, legal exceptions, technical limitations, audit obligations, and retention requirements.

## 17. Cookies and Similar Technologies

ORSA may use cookies, tokens, and similar technologies to keep users signed in, remember settings, support authentication flows, secure sessions, and improve reliability.

Users can control some browser technologies through browser settings, but disabling them may break account, chat, upload, or settings features.

## 18. Location and Camera/File Permissions

ORSA may request browser location to capture country, region/state, or city during signup or profile workflows. ORSA may use IP fallback when browser geolocation is unavailable.

ORSA may use camera/file permissions only when users choose to capture or upload files. Users can revoke browser permissions through their device or browser settings.

## 19. Children and Minors

ORSA is not directed to children. ORSA should not knowingly collect information from children below the age required by applicable law without appropriate parent or guardian involvement and compliance controls.

If ORSA learns that it collected child information without required authorization, it will take appropriate steps to delete or restrict the information.

## 20. Automated Decision-Making

ORSA provides AI-assisted guidance and triage support. It should not be used as the sole basis for decisions that produce legal, medical, insurance, employment, educational, or similarly significant effects.

Qualified human review is required for medical diagnosis, treatment, prescription, emergency decisions, and other high-risk decisions.

## 21. De-Identified and Aggregated Data

ORSA may create de-identified, anonymized, or aggregated data for analytics, quality, safety, security, research, or product improvement where permitted.

ORSA should not attempt to re-identify data that has been de-identified except where necessary to validate de-identification, investigate misuse, or comply with law.

## 22. Marketing

ORSA should not send marketing communications, newsletters, or promotional messages unless a lawful basis and opt-out mechanism are implemented.

ORSA should not use personal health information for targeted advertising without a separate legally compliant framework.

## 23. Changes to This Policy

ORSA may update this Privacy Policy. Versioned policies may be displayed during signup, accessible later, stored with acceptance records, and linked to consent history.

If changes are material, ORSA may provide notice or request renewed acceptance where required.

## 24. Contact

Privacy questions, rights requests, security concerns, and deletion requests should be sent to the ORSA privacy/support contact configured for the deployed service.

Production deployments should replace this placeholder with a real privacy email, support email, postal address, legal entity name, and jurisdiction-specific contact details.
